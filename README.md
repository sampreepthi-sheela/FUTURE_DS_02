# FUTURE_DS_02
Customer Retention &amp; Churn Analysis

Project Overview:

This project focuses on analyzing customer churn and retention patterns in a subscription-based business.
The goal is to understand why customers leave, identify high-risk segments, and provide actionable recommendations to improve retention and business growth.

Business Questions Addressed:
Why are customers leaving the platform?
Which customer segments are most likely to churn?
How long do customers typically stay active?
What actions can improve customer retention?

Tools Used:
Tableau
Data Cleaning & Preparation
KPI Analysis

Key Metrics (KPIs):
Total Customers
Churned Customers
Churn Rate (%)
Retention Rate (%)

Analysis Performed:
1. Churn by Contract

Analyzed how contract type affects churn behavior.

2. Churn by Tenure

Studied how customer lifetime impacts churn.

3. Churn by Category

Identified high-risk customer segments based on services and payment methods.

4. Monthly Charges vs Churn

Examined the impact of pricing on customer retention.

Key Insights:
Customers with month-to-month contracts have the highest churn rates
Most customers leave during the early stages (low tenure)
Higher monthly charges are associated with increased churn
Long-term customers are more stable and less likely to churn

Business Recommendations:
Encourage long-term subscriptions through discounts or incentives
Improve onboarding experience to reduce early churn
Optimize pricing strategies for better customer retention
Target high-risk customer segments with personalized engagement

Conclusion:
This project demonstrates how data analysis can help businesses understand customer behavior, reduce churn, and improve long-term growth through data-driven decision-making.
